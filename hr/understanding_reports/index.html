    Understanding Candidate Reports¶

    After VICA ranks candidates, it generates a detailed report for each shortlisted candidate. This guide explains every section of the report and how to use it in your hiring process.

    Report Overview¶

    The report compares one candidate against one job. It shows: - How well the candidate's skills match the job requirements - Evidence from the CV supporting each skill - Where the candidate is strong, and where there are gaps - Targeted interview questions to verify claims

    What the report does NOT do: It does not assign a numerical "fit score out of 10". It does not make a hiring decision. It provides evidence and analysis to support your judgment.

    Recommendation Badge¶

    At the top of the report, you'll see one of four badges:

    Badge What it means What to do
    Strong Yes Excellent fit — strong evidence across required skills Move to interview, high priority
    Yes Good fit — meets most requirements with solid evidence Move to interview, standard priority
    Maybe Mixed signals — some gaps or unsubstantiated claims Review carefully, consider if gaps are dealbreakers
    No Not suitable — missing critical required skills Deprioritize unless circumstances change

    Overall Fit¶

    A 1-2 sentence assessment summarizing the candidate's fit. Read this first for a quick impression before diving into details.

    Strengths & Risks¶

    Two bullet-point lists:

    • Strengths (green): 2-4 key things going for this candidate. These are the reasons to interview them.
    • Risks / Gaps (red): 1-3 concerns. These are areas to probe in the interview or reasons for caution.

    How to use: Strengths validate the candidate. Risks become the basis for your interview questions — if VICA flags a gap, ask about it.

    Required Skills Table¶

    A table with one row per required job skill. Each row shows:

    Column What it means
    Skill name The required skill from your job description
    Status Check mark (has it) or cross (doesn't have it)
    Match level How well the candidate matches this specific skill
    Evidence Summary of relevant CV content

    Match Level Badges¶

    Badge Meaning Typical action
    Strong match (green) Clear direct experience with this skill No concerns for this skill
    Partial match (yellow) Some evidence but not deep, or related but not exact Ask about depth in interview
    Transferable (blue) Has a closely related skill that could transfer Ask about willingness/ability to transition
    No match (red) No evidence of this or related skills Major gap — assess if trainable or dealbreaker

    CV Citations¶

    Under each skill assessment, you may see "CV Citations:" followed by exact quotes from the candidate's CV. These are: - Copied verbatim from the CV (not paraphrased) - Full sentences or bullet points (not fragments) - From multiple CV sections if the skill appears in different places

    Why this matters: Citations let you verify the AI's assessment. If a skill is marked "strong_match", the citations should show real project experience. If citations seem thin, the match might be weaker than it appears.

    Preferred Skills Table¶

    Same format as the required skills table, but for "nice to have" skills. These skills carry less weight in the ranking but can differentiate between similar candidates.

    Transferable Skills¶

    Cards showing skills the candidate has that were not in your job requirements but are relevant. Each card shows:

    • Skill name: The candidate's skill
    • Relevant to: Which job skill(s) it could complement or substitute
    • Reasoning: Why this skill is transferable or valuable

    How to use: Transferable skills can be a tiebreaker. A candidate with "PostgreSQL" experience when you asked for "SQL" has transferable knowledge. A candidate with "project management" when you didn't ask for it might bring unexpected value.

    Interview Questions¶

    4-8 targeted questions designed to help you interview this specific candidate. Questions come in two categories:

    Verification Questions (blue badge)¶

    These probe skills the candidate claimed but with little supporting evidence. They help you determine whether the candidate really has these skills.

    Example: "You list Kubernetes in your skills section but your CV doesn't describe any container orchestration projects. Can you describe a specific production deployment you managed with Kubernetes?"

    When to use: Always ask verification questions — they catch inflated CVs.

    Technical Depth Questions (orange badge)¶

    These test the depth of the candidate's strongest skills. They go beyond what the CV describes to see if the candidate has real expertise.

    Example: "You architected the migration from monolith to microservices at CompanyX. How did you handle distributed transactions across services, and what trade-offs did you make?"

    When to use: Use these to differentiate between candidates who look similar on paper.

    Priority Badges¶

    Each question has a priority:

    Priority Meaning
    High Critical to ask — addresses a key uncertainty
    Medium Important — helps complete the picture
    Low Nice to have — for deeper exploration if time allows

    Additional Skills¶

    A list of skills the candidate has that were not part of your job requirements or transferable skills. Shows skill name and proficiency level. Useful for understanding the candidate's broader profile.

    Using Reports in Your Process¶

    1. First pass: Read the recommendation badge and overall fit for each candidate
    2. Compare: Look at required skills tables side by side — who has more green badges?
    3. Prepare interviews: Use the interview questions directly or as inspiration
    4. Verify: Check CV citations for skills you care most about
    5. Decide: Use strengths/risks to justify your shortlist decisions

    Remember: VICA assists your judgment. The final decision is always yours.
