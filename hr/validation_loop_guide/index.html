    Validation Loop — Step-by-Step Guide¶

    What is Validation?¶

    Validation lets you measure how well VICA's ranking matches real hiring outcomes. You can answer questions like: - "If we had used VICA for our last hiring round, would it have recommended the same candidates we interviewed?" - "Does VICA's ranking agree with my expert judgment?"

    This helps you build confidence in the system and tune it over time.

    Two Modes¶

    Historical Validation¶

    Use past hiring data where you already know who was interviewed and hired. Upload the CVs from a completed hiring round, let VICA rank them, then compare its ranking to your actual decisions.

    Best for: Backtesting, building stakeholder confidence, measuring accuracy over time.

    Supervised Validation¶

    Run a side-by-side comparison: you independently pick your top candidates, then VICA ranks them separately. Compare the two shortlists.

    Best for: Current hiring rounds, calibrating VICA against your judgment, identifying blind spots.

    Step-by-Step: Creating a Validation Run¶

    1. Navigate to Validation¶

    From the left sidebar, click Validation. You'll see a list of any existing validation runs.

    2. Create a New Run¶

    1. Click "New Validation Run"
    2. Enter a title (e.g., "Q1 2026 Backend Hiring" or "Senior Engineer - March")
    3. Choose the type:
    4. Historical — you know who was interviewed/hired
    5. Supervised — you'll make independent picks
    6. Click Create

    3. Upload Candidates¶

    1. In the validation run detail view, upload CVs (same as the regular hiring loop)
    2. Click "Parse All" to extract text from CVs
    3. Click "Extract Skills" to identify skills from each CV
    4. Wait for all candidates to show green "Ready" badges

    4. Set the Job Description¶

    1. Paste or upload the job description text
    2. Click "Save JD"
    3. The system extracts skills from the JD
    4. Review and validate the skills (reorder, toggle required/preferred, delete irrelevant ones)
    5. Click "Save Skills"

    5a. Historical: Mark Past Outcomes¶

    If you chose Historical mode:

    1. For each candidate, set their actual outcome using the status selector:
    2. Interviewed — was interviewed (whether or not they were hired)
    3. Hired — was ultimately hired
    4. Rejected — was not selected for interview
    5. These outcomes are your ground truth — what actually happened

    Tip: You don't need to mark every candidate. The ones you mark as "interviewed" or "hired" are the positive set. Everyone else is treated as negative.

    5b. Supervised: Make Your Picks¶

    If you chose Supervised mode:

    1. Review the candidates (you can look at CVs, but not at VICA's ranking — it hasn't run yet)
    2. Select the candidates you would shortlist based on your expert judgment
    3. Save your picks

    Important: Make your picks BEFORE running the match. The comparison is only meaningful if your picks are independent of VICA's ranking.

    6. Run the Match¶

    1. Click "Run Match"
    2. VICA ranks ALL candidates using SPEA2
    3. Then analyzes the top candidates with the AI
    4. Progress is streamed — you'll see "Ranking..." then "Analyzing candidate 1/N..."

    7. Compute Statistics¶

    1. After the match completes, click "Compute Stats"
    2. VICA compares its top-K ranking against your ground truth and calculates:

    Key metrics explained in plain language:

    Metric What it answers
    Precision "Of the candidates VICA picked, how many did you also pick?"
    Recall "Of the candidates you picked, how many did VICA find?"
    F1 Score Combined measure of precision and recall (higher = better agreement)
    Accuracy@K "What fraction of VICA's top-K are actual positives?"

    Example: If you interviewed 5 candidates and VICA's top 5 includes 3 of them: - Precision = ⅗ = 60% ("3 of VICA's 5 picks were correct") - Recall = ⅗ = 60% ("VICA found 3 of the 5 you interviewed") - F1 = 60%

    8. View the Stats Report¶

    Click "View Report" to see a detailed HTML report including: - Core metrics (Precision, Recall, F1, Accuracy@K) - Rank analysis: Where did your actual picks land in VICA's full ranking? (average rank, best rank, worst rank) - Skill coverage: How many candidates have each skill - Fitness distribution: Are ground-truth candidates scoring higher than others? - True/False Positives: Which candidates VICA got right vs wrong

    9. Aggregate Report¶

    If you've run multiple validation rounds, click "Aggregate Report" to see: - Average precision, recall, F1 across all runs - Standard deviation (consistency measure) - Total candidates evaluated and total ground truth size

    Interpreting Results¶

    What "good" metrics look like¶

    Metric Good Okay Needs improvement
    Precision > 70% 50-70% < 50%
    Recall > 70% 50-70% < 50%
    F1 > 70% 50-70% < 50%

    Common situations¶

    High precision, low recall: VICA is conservative — the candidates it picks are good, but it's missing some that you would have picked. Consider increasing the top-K size.

    Low precision, high recall: VICA is recommending too broadly — it finds all your candidates but also includes many you wouldn't pick. The skill ordering or required/preferred classification may need tuning.

    Low precision AND low recall: The skill list may not match what you're actually evaluating. Review the job skills — are the right skills marked as required? Is the ordering correct?

    What to adjust¶

    • Skill order: Move the most important skills to the top
    • Required vs preferred: Make sure critical skills are "required"
    • Skill list: Add missing skills or remove irrelevant ones
    • Top-K size: Adjust how many candidates VICA shortlists

    After adjusting, run a new validation to see if metrics improve.

    For the mathematical definitions of all metrics, see Validation Metrics.
