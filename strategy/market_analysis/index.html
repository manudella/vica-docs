    Tier 4 is virtually unserved

    OpenCATS has no AI capabilities and hasn't had a significant release in years. VICA is the first entrant combining on-premise deployment with AI screening.

    Growth Drivers¶

    • Skills-based hiring replacing credential-based — 40% YoY growth in skills-first postings (LinkedIn)
    • 250 applications per job — manual screening is economically unsustainable
    • Remote work expanding candidate pools globally, compounding volume + data residency complexity
    • DEI mandates driving demand for auditable, bias-reducing AI
    • AI regulation (EU AI Act, NYC LL144, Colorado AI Act) creating mandatory compliance spend

    Privacy & Regulatory Advantage¶

    EU AI Act — Employment AI as High-Risk¶

    timeline
    title EU AI Act Compliance Timeline
    August 2024 : Regulation enters into force
    February 2025 : Prohibited AI practices apply
    August 2025 : New AI systems must comply
    August 2026 : General-purpose AI obligations
    August 2027 : Existing high-risk systems must comply

    Employment AI is explicitly listed as high-risk (Annex III, 4a). Penalties: up to €35M or 7% of global turnover.

    EU AI Act Requirement VICA's Answer
    Transparency (Art. 13) Citation-level evidence per skill assessment
    Human oversight (Art. 14) Recruiter validation loop (drag-drop skill ordering)
    Risk management (Art. 9) Open-source, auditable algorithm
    Accuracy (Art. 15) Built-in Precision/Recall/F1 framework
    Technical docs (Art. 11) This documentation

    SWOT Analysis¶

    Strengths¶

    • Complete data sovereignty — no third-party processors
    • Citation-level explainability (unmatched)
    • SPEA2 multi-objective ranking (Pareto-optimal)
    • Open-source, fully auditable
    • Built-in validation framework
    • Zero recurring license fees

    Weaknesses¶

    • Requires internal DevOps capability
    • JSON file storage (not enterprise-grade)
    • No native ATS workflow features
    • Limited integrations
    • Single-GPU throughput constraint
    • No commercial support

    Opportunities¶

    • EU AI Act enforcement (2025–2027)
    • GDPR enforcement intensifying (€4.5B+ fines)
    • Growing on-premise AI demand in regulated industries
    • Consulting firms as beachhead market
    • ATS integration partnerships
    • Model upgrades (70B class) without architecture changes

    Threats¶

    • Cloud vendors adding hybrid/on-premise options
    • Competitors adding explainability features
    • Rapid AI model evolution
    • "Good enough" cloud solutions for unregulated firms
    • Internal IT resistance to self-hosted solutions
    • Market consolidation
